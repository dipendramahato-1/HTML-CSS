Prithvi Narayan Shah

1723-1775

Founder of Modern Nepal

Prithvi Narayan Shah (1723 – 11 January 1775) was the first king of unified Nepal and is widely regarded as the founder of modern Nepal. He was born in Gorkha and was the son of King Nara Bhupal Shah. From an early age, he showed strong leadership qualities and a clear vision of unifying the small kingdoms of Nepal. He studied statecraft, military strategy, and politics, which later helped him in his campaigns. Prithvi Narayan Shah spent much of his life expanding his kingdom by conquering neighboring states. His most important achievement was the unification of the Kathmandu Valley, which included the kingdoms of Kantipur, Lalitpur, and Bhaktapur. He is famous for his policy known as “Dibya Upadesh,” where he described Nepal as a “yam between two boulders,” referring to India and China. He emphasized self-reliance, strong defense, and careful foreign relations. Through his leadership, determination, and strategic planning, he laid the foundation of a unified Nepal. His contributions to the nation are remembered as a turning point in Nepalese history.
